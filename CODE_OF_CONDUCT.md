# Code Of Conduct

Wayfindr should be a practical, respectful project for people building and using open source support software.

Expected behavior:

- Be clear and constructive.
- Assume public discussion will be read by future users and contributors.
- Criticize ideas and implementations, not people.
- Keep issues and pull requests focused on the project.
- Do not publish private user, customer, or business information.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, personal attacks, deliberate disruption, and disclosure of private information.

Until a full enforcement process exists, maintainers may moderate, lock, or remove project spaces that violate these expectations. Formal reporting and appeal details should be published before the project opens to broader community contribution.
